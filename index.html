Technical Breakdown: How Adesanya Finally Solved the Pereira Puzzle at UFC 287

JD

John Doe

UFC Analyst & Former Fighter

Israel Adesanya's knockout victory over Alex Pereira at UFC 287 wasn't just redemption - it was a masterclass in tactical adjustment. After three previous encounters where Pereira had Adesanya's number, "The Last Stylebender" made crucial changes that turned the tables decisively in Miami.

The Footwork Revolution

The most noticeable difference came in Adesanya's footwork. Historically, Adesanya fought Pereira moving backward, conceding ground to the powerful Brazilian kickboxer. This time, Adesanya constantly circled to his right (Pereira's left), staying away from Pereira's vaunted left hook while maintaining angle control.

Statistical analysis shows Adesanya threw 63% of his strikes while moving laterally compared to just 28% in their previous MMA fight. This constant movement prevented Pereira from setting his feet for power shots while allowing Adesanya to dart in with quick combinations.

The Setup That Changed Everything

The finishing sequence didn't come out of nowhere. Adesanya spent rounds perfecting his right hand setup, consistently throwing a right kick to the body that Pereira would check with his left arm. By Round 2, Pereira's defensive reaction became predictable.

At 4:21 of Round 2, Adesanya threw the body kick, Pereira lifted his left arm to block, and Adesanya immediately feinted another kick before unleashing the right hand that would end the fight. Pereira's left arm was out of position due to the expected kick defense, leaving his chin completely exposed.
